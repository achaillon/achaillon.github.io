It is very clear the APRV when applied correctly has many benefits to improve patient outcome. Not only does it increase gas exchange efficiency (both oxygenation and ventilation) improving V/Q matching, Crs, and lung aeration homogeneity while using a lower mechanical power, it improves systemic hemodynamics reducing vasopressor requirements and is more comfortable allowing patients to be awake and mobilized. This MV strategy should be popularized as it has the potential to reduce mortality and need for ECMO, while also shortening LOS in ICU and on MV.
