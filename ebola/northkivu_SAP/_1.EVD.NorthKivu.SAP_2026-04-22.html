Data Mapping & Validation

To ensure the integrity of the secondary analysis, a data dictionary mapping exercise will be conducted:

  • Variable Alignment: REDCap field names from the EVD project (e.g., diarrhoea_anytime) will be mapped to all the variables from the North Kivu outbreak .
  • Harmonization Check: Discrepancies in units (e.g., lab values) or categorical definitions will be resolved and documented in a transparent mapping_logfile.csv to ensure reproducibility.
  • Plausibility Filters: Automated checks will flag biologically implausible values (e.g., Ct values <10 or >45) for manual review or exclusion.

1. Study Overview

This SAP outlines the proposed analysis of the 756 defined patients admitted between August 2018 and July 2019 during the North Kivu EVD outbreak. The goal is to identify clinical and laboratory determinants of mortality, with a sub analysis on pediatric populations. ## 1.1 Study Flow Diagram(Figure 1)

2. Variable Grouping & Data Harmonization

To analyze the predictors of EVD outcome and complications, we propose this Directed Acyclic Graphs (DAG) (Figure 2) to illustrate hypothesized causal relationships underlying EVD pathophysiology and clinical outcomes.

To ensure model parsimony, we will group sparse clinical variables into syndromic categories (Table 1):

  • CNS Signs: Agitation, coma, disorientation, seizures, and irritability.

  • GI Signs: Diarrhea, vomiting, abdominal pain, and anorexia.

  • Renal/Dehydration: Anuria, signs of dehydration, and renal impairment (Creatinine ≥ 1.1 mg/dL).

3. Statistical Methodology

We propose a stepwise modeling workflow (Figure 3) to identify independent predictors of clinical outcomes and complications.

3.1 Data Preparation and Harmonization

Data cleaning and wrangling are performed using the tidyverse ecosystem.(Wickham et al. 2019) All clinical variables are inspected for consistency, and missingness is addressed through complete-case analysis or multiple imputation, depending on the variable’s importance and the proportion of missing entries.

3.2 Predictor Selection Framework

We will utilize a structured workflow to identify independent predictors:

  1. Univariate screening : Each potential predictor (demographics, symptoms, and lab values) is evaluated in univariate models. Variables with extreme sparsity or those causing quasi-complete separation are excluded from further modeling.

  2. Collinearity assessment: Multicollinearity among remaining variables is assessed using the car package. (Fox and Weisberg 2019)

  3. LASSO regression: Least Absolute Shrinkage and Selection Operator (LASSO) regression is applied via the glmnet package. (Friedman, Hastie, and Tibshirani 2010) This penalized regression technique shrinks coefficients of weaker predictors to zero, retaining only the most stable clinical associations for the final multivariable models.

3.3 Multivariable Modeling and Effect Estimation

The final analytic models will quantify the relationship between selected predictors and EVD outcomes:

  • Primary Effect Estimation: To enhance clinical interpretability, we will report Adjusted Risk Ratios (aRR) and 95% confidence intervals (CI). These are derived using Poisson regression with robust standard errors (via the sandwich and lmtest packages [Zeileis, Koll, and Graham (2020)](Zeileis and Hothorn 2002) ), which provides more reliable estimates than odds ratios in settings where the outcome is not rare.

  • Sensitivity Analysis: For comparison with historical literature, Adjusted Odds Ratios (aOR) will be computed via global multivariable logistic regression.

  • Prognostic Thresholds: For continuous laboratory markers such as Cycle Threshold (Ct) values, we will utilize Receiver Operating Characteristic (ROC) analysis to determine optimal prognostic cut-offs (e.g., Ct \(\approx 23\)) for predicting mortality.

3.4 Software and Reproducibility

All analyses are conducted in R version 4.4.1. The use of a scripted, Quarto-based workflow ensures that the transition from the “Light CRF” raw data to the final analytical outputs is fully reproducible and transparent.

4. Pediatric Sub-analysis (Proposed)

Given the demographic distribution, we propose a dedicated focus on the XX pediatric cases:

  • Comparison of Clinical Phenotypes

  • Survival Comparison: Weibull proportional hazards models stratified by age groups (<5, 5-17) to determine if admission delays impact children differently than adults.

5. Proposed Analytical Outputs

The following outputs are proposed to characterize the North Kivu cohort and identify key clinical determinants of EVD outcomes.

Expected Output 1: Table 1 - Patient Demographics & Baseline Features

A comprehensive descriptive table stratified by Age Group (Adult vs. Children), Outcome (Deceased vs. Recovered), and Sex (Male vs. Female).

Variables included:

  • Demographics: Age at admission (Continuous), Age Group (Categorical), Gender, Patient Occupation, Pregnancy Status.

  • Admission Data: Admission Delay (Days), Comorbid conditions (Diabetes, HIV, Chronic Pulmonary Disease).

  • Baseline Status: Fever, Diarrhoea, Shock, Confusion, Bleeding (Binary).

  • Lab/Diagnostic: Ebola RDT, GeneXpert PCR, Ct (lowest), Ct Below 23, Electrolytes (Hypo/Hyperkaliemia), Renal impairment, Rhabdomyolysis, Severe hypoglycemia, Platelets, Haemoglobin, Calcium.

  • Initial Management: Experimental treatment type, Antibacterial/Antimalarial treatment, Fluid management, Blood transfusion.

Expected Output 2: Epicurve and Case Fatality Rate (CFR)

Visualization of the outbreak progression by week of admission, with bar colors representing Outcome at Discharge. A secondary axis will plot the rolling CFR to monitor changes in mortality over time.

Expected Output 3: Symptom & Complication Prevalence Maps

Bar charts or Heatmaps summarizing the frequency of the following binary clinical features:

  • Symptoms: Abdominal pain, Agitation, Anorexia, Anuria, Chest Pain, Disorientation, Diarrhoea, Difficulty breathing, Dysphagia, Fatigue, Fever, Headache, Hiccups, Myalgia, Seizure, Signs of shock/dehydration.
  • Clinical Signs: Conjunctival injection, Lymphadenopathy, Jaundice, Oedema (face/neck), Palpable liver, Pharyngeal erythema, Rash, Sunken eyes, Tenting on skin pinch.
  • Severe Complications: Severe thrombocytopenia, Hyper/Hypokaliemia, Renal impairment, Rhabdomyolysis, Severe hypoglycemia.
Expected Output 4: Prognostic Association Analysis

Visualizations and summary tables for clinical predictors of mortality:

  1. Outcome by Admission Delay: Analysis of survival probability based on the time from symptom onset to ETU admission.
  2. Outcome by Viral Load (Ct): Stratification of mortality risk by admission Ct values (e.g., <16, 16-18, 18-20, 20-22).
  3. Predictor Forest Plot: Presentation of Relative Risks (RR) and 95% CIs for key variables:
    • Continuous: Ct at Admission, Admission Delay, Days from Treatment Initiation.
    • Categorical: Specific Experimental Treatments (Remdesivir, Zmapp, mAb114), Presence of Bleeding Signs, Signs of Renal Impairment, Rhabdomyolysis.
